Weijie Wang

I am a robotics researcher working on humanoid and legged-robot locomotion, loco-manipulation, and learning-based control and planning. I am especially interested in grounding vision–language models (VLMs) and large language models (LLMs) into embodied decision-making, so that robots can act adaptively in unstructured, real-world environments.

From Nov 2023 to Nov 2025 I was a Researcher at the Italian Institute of Technology (IIT) in Genoa, Italy, where I worked on autonomous whole-body loco-manipulation and VLM-grounded motion intention. I received my M.S. in Electronic Information from the University of Chinese Academy of Sciences (UCAS), carrying out research at the Institute of Automation, CAS on highly dynamic biped locomotion and safe motion planning.

Publications

Author name shown in bold.

INTENTION: Inferring Tendencies of Humanoid Robot Motion Through Interactive Intuition and Grounded VLM
Jin Wang, Weijie Wang, Boyuan Deng, Heng Zhang, Rui Dai, Nikos Tsagarakis
IEEE-RAS International Conference on Humanoid Robots (Humanoids), 2025
arXiv / project page / IEEE

A framework that gives robots interactive intuition for autonomous manipulation by coupling VLM-based scene reasoning with an interaction-driven Memory Graph and an Intuitive Perceptor that extracts physical relations and affordances—letting robots infer suitable behaviors in novel scenes without repeated instructions.

HYPERmotion: Learning Hybrid Behavior Planning for Autonomous Loco-manipulation
Jin Wang, Rui Dai, Weijie Wang, Luca Rossini, Francesco Ruscelli, Nikos Tsagarakis
Conference on Robot Learning (CoRL), 2024
arXiv / project page / proceedings

A framework that learns, selects and plans whole-body behaviors across scenarios. It combines reinforcement learning with whole-body optimization over 38 actuated joints, stores skills in a motion library, and uses LLMs to build a hierarchical task graph while a VLM grounds perception into a morphology selector for single/dual-arm and legged/wheeled locomotion.

Highly Dynamic Locomotion Control of Biped Robot with Swing Arms
Weijie Wang, Song Liu, Qinfeng Shan, Lihao Jia
IEEE International Conference on Advanced Robotics and Mechatronics (ICARM), 2023
arXiv / IEEE

A control strategy that integrates swing arms into the full jumping pipeline—modeling, trajectory planning and tracking—by modeling the biped as a Flywheel Dynamics Spring Model and realizing it with a whole-body controller. On the Purple V1.0 robot, swing arms improved agility, roughly halved stabilization time, and reduced energy consumption.

Improving the Ability of Robots to Navigate Through Crowded Environments Safely using Deep Reinforcement Learning
Weijie Wang, et al.
IEEE International Conference on Advanced Robotics and Mechatronics (ICARM), 2022
IEEE

A safe and efficient motion-planning method that pairs a forward-reachable-set formulation (offline) with deep reinforcement learning (online decision-making), raising the success rate of passing through crowded, narrow environments compared with reachability-only baselines.
